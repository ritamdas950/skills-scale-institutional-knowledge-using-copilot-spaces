# OctoAcme — Stakeholder Engagement Guide

## Purpose
Provide a framework for identifying stakeholders, understanding their needs, and maintaining effective communication throughout the project lifecycle.

## Stakeholder Identification

### When to identify stakeholders
- During project initiation
- Before planning begins
- When scope or deliverables change significantly

### Stakeholder Categories

**Primary Stakeholders**
- Direct beneficiaries or impacted parties
- Business owners and decision-makers
- End users (when applicable)

**Secondary Stakeholders**
- Teams providing dependencies or support
- Cross-functional partners (marketing, support, ops)
- Subject matter experts

**Tertiary Stakeholders**
- Leadership and governance
- Compliance and risk teams
- External partners or vendors

## Stakeholder Analysis Template

| Name | Role | Interest | Influence | Engagement Strategy |
|------|------|----------|-----------|---------------------|
| | | High/Med/Low | High/Med/Low | |

**Engagement Strategy Options:**
- **Keep Satisfied**: High influence, medium interest → Regular updates
- **Manage Closely**: High influence, high interest → Active collaboration
- **Keep Informed**: Low influence, medium interest → Status reports
- **Monitor**: Low influence, low interest → Minimal engagement

## Communication Planning

### Define Communication Plan
1. **Stakeholder group**: Identify primary audience
2. **Message**: What information is critical for this group?
3. **Frequency**: Weekly, milestone-based, ad-hoc?
4. **Channel**: Email, meeting, dashboard, Slack?
5. **Owner**: Who is responsible for communication?

### Communication Templates

**Executive Status Update**
```
Project: [Name]
Reporting Period: [Dates]

Status: [On Track / At Risk / Off Track]

Key Highlights:
- Accomplishment 1
- Accomplishment 2

Risks & Blockers:
- Risk 1 (Impact, Mitigation)
- Blocker 1 (Action, Owner)

Next Steps:
- Milestone for next period
- Decision needed?

Budget/Timeline: [Status vs. plan]
```

**Business Owner Briefing**
```
Project: [Name]
Period: [Dates]

Business Metrics:
- [Metric 1]: [Status] vs. [Target]
- [Metric 2]: [Status] vs. [Target]

Deliverables On Track:
- Feature 1: On schedule
- Feature 2: On schedule

Business Risks:
- Risk and mitigation

Resourced Needed:
- Ask/Decision
```

## Stakeholder Feedback Integration

### Feedback Channels
- Steering committee meetings
- Retrospectives and lessons learned
- User research and validation sessions
- Post-release outcome reviews

### Feedback Loop
1. Collect feedback systematically
2. Analyze and prioritize feedback
3. Incorporate into backlog or process improvements
4. Report back on actions taken

## Stakeholder Engagement Checklist
- [ ] Stakeholders identified and categorized
- [ ] Communication plan documented
- [ ] Stakeholders briefed on project goals and timeline
- [ ] Regular touchpoints scheduled
- [ ] Feedback mechanisms established
- [ ] Decisions and approvals tracked
- [ ] Post-release feedback captured

## Escalation & Decision-Making

### Decision Authority Matrix
| Decision Type | Authority | Timeline | Escalation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Scope changes | Product Lead + Sponsor | 2 business days | PM to Sponsor |
| Timeline extension | Project Manager + Sponsor | 1 business day | PM to Sponsor |
| Resource trade-offs | Sponsor | 1 business day | PM escalates |
| Go/no-go release | Sponsor + Product Lead | 1 business day | PM facilitates |

### Decision Documentation
- Record who made the decision
- Document the rationale
- Capture trade-offs and alternatives considered
- Archive for project closeout
